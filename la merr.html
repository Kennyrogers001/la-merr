The "la merr" strategy is a technical analysis approach for swing trading blue-chip stocks or ETFs. Here's a summary:


1. Indicators Used:

  • RSI (Relative Strength Index): 14-period
  • EMA (Exponential Moving Averages): 5, 10, and 20 periods

2. Entry Point:

  • Buy when the EMA 5 crosses above EMA 20.

3. Exit Strategy:

  • First Exit: Sell 20% of the position when EMA 5 crosses below EMA 10.
  • Second Exit: Sell 40% of the position when EMA 10 crosses below EMA 20.
  • Hold the remaining 40% indefinitely.

4. Conditions:

  • No trades if the RSI is at or above 70 (overbought condition).

5. Special Rule:

  • The first exit (20% sell) occurs only once. If EMA 5 crosses EMA 10 more than once before EMA 10 crosses EMA 20, only the second exit (40% sell) is valid.

This strategy focuses on capturing gains from trends while managing risk through staged exits.
